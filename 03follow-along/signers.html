Who Were the Signers

Most Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence. Later served as the 3rd President of the U.S. and founded the University of Virginia. He is remembered as one of the greatest intellectual forces behind American independence.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
A leading advocate for independence in Congress. He became the 2nd President of the U.S. and was the first Vice President under George Washington. Known for his fierce independence and central role in founding the nation.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned diplomat, scientist, inventor, and writer. He served as U.S. Ambassador to France and helped negotiate the Treaty of Paris. A true Renaissance man, Franklin was perhaps the most universally admired American of his time.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
Known for his large and bold signature on the Declaration—his name became synonymous with “signature.” He was President of the Second Continental Congress and later Governor of Massachusetts.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
A leader of the Sons of Liberty and key instigator of the Boston Tea Party. He later served as Governor of Massachusetts and was one of the most radical and outspoken revolutionaries.
Richard Henry Lee (Virginia)
Introduced the Lee Resolution calling for independence from Britain. He later served as President of the Continental Congress and U.S. Senator. Credited with sparking the formal move toward declaring independence.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
The only person to sign all four great state papers of the U.S.: the Continental Association, Declaration of Independence, Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution. A key legal and political thinker.
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer of the Declaration and the last surviving signer (died in 1832). He was a wealthy planter and advocate for religious liberty.
Thomas Nelson Jr. (Virginia)
A patriot general during the Revolutionary War who famously ordered artillery to fire on his own home when it was occupied by British forces. He later served as Governor of Virginia.
