Rappelling Overview

Introduction

Rappelling, also known as abseiling, is the controlled descent down a rock face or cliff using a rope. It is commonly practiced in canyoneering, rock climbing, mountaineering, and rescue operations. Utah’s red rock country provides some of the most spectacular locations in the world for rappelling and slot canyon exploration.

Popular Locations in Utah

  • Zion National Park – iconic sandstone canyons and technical slot routes
  • Moab – desert towers, arches, and scenic rappels
  • Grand Staircase–Escalante – remote slot canyons
  • Capitol Reef – colorful cliffs and narrow slots

Audience

Red Rock Rappels is designed for adults interested in canyoneering, outdoor adventurers, and guides looking for information on Utah’s slot canyons and rappelling routes.

Modern Practices

With modern gear and safety practices, rappelling has become more accessible than ever. High-quality ropes, helmets, and rappel devices ensure safety, while GPS navigation and guidebooks help adventurers find new routes. While skilled guides and climbers pursue technical descents, many recreational visitors enjoy shorter, guided rappelling experiences.
