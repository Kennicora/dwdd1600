Rappelling Overview

Introduction

Rappelling, also known as abseiling, is a controlled descent down a rock face using a rope. It's widely practiced in canyoneering, mountaineering, and rescue operations.

Utah’s red rock country offers some of the world’s most iconic spots for rappelling and slot canyon exploration. Adventurers from around the world come here for its unique beauty and challenge.

Popular Locations in Utah

  1. Zion National Park – Iconic sandstone canyons and technical slot routes
  2. Moab – Desert towers, arches, and scenic rappels
  3. Grand Staircase–Escalante – Remote slot canyons
  4. Capitol Reef – Colorful cliffs and narrow slots

These areas offer options for beginners and experienced climbers alike. Make sure to check conditions before planning your trip.

Glossary

Rappel
A controlled descent using a rope down a rock face.
Slot Canyon
A narrow canyon formed by water erosion, often with rappelling routes.
Anchor
A secure point used to attach the rope for descending.
